Docling OCR — Multilingual Document Processing
Issue #123 · Repository

Tested Docling's OCR capabilities across three engines — Tesseract, EasyOCR, and ocrmac (Apple Vision) — on French, Italian, and Telugu documents. Key insight: font quality matters more than language support.

French and Italian converted cleanly across all four engines
Old Telugu manuscripts failed on every engine — font quality matters more than language support
EasyOCR unexpectedly outperformed Tesseract on modern Telugu
ocrmac (Apple Vision) doesn't support Indic scripts at all
RapidOCR is fast and needs no language flag — but struggles with Indic scripts
Skipping --ocr-lang flag noticeably hurt accuracy

RamaLama — Getting Started with AI Models

Installed RamaLama 0.18.0 on macOS (Apple Silicon M2), tested two models across two transports — ollama://tinyllama and huggingface://TheBloke/TinyLlama — using Fedora-specific prompts to evaluate accuracy.

Docling CLI — PDF Processing & Format Comparison
Issue #122 · Repository

Used Docling CLI to convert a complex academic PDF (DocLayNet research paper) into Markdown, HTML, JSON, and DocTags. Compared 7 different options including pipelines, table modes, OCR settings, and layout flags.

Markdown is best for RAG — clean, compact and LLM-friendly with no noisy tags
JSON exposes bounding boxes, page numbers and content labels — ideal for custom chunking
--no-ocr is 2x faster with zero quality loss on digitally created PDFs
--table-mode accurate essential for complex tables — fast mode loses column headers entirely
VLM pipeline (30-40 min/page) only needed for scanned docs — standard pipeline handles digital PDFs perfectly

What I Learned
What I Learned
Open source felt overwhelming — then it clickedSetting up FAS, finding the right channels, making my first issue comment. Once the mentor responded, I realized open source is just people helping each other.
I learned more by doing than by readingRunning four OCR engines on real Telugu manuscripts and watching them fail taught me more than any documentation could about the gap between what AI claims to support and what it actually handles.
Every task connected to something biggerDocling parses the document, RamaLama runs the model, RAG connects them. I wasn't just completing tasks — I was building understanding of a real AI pipeline piece by piece.
My background became a contributionTesting Telugu wasn't just technical. As someone who grew up with the language, seeing powerful AI tools fail on it meant something personal — and my mentor noticed and valued that perspective.
This changed how I see myself as a contributorBefore Outreachy I thought open source was for experienced developers. Now I know clear documentation, honest testing, and genuine curiosity are just as valuable as code.
